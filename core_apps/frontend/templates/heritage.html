Our Heritage

Mill Bakers Ltd was started as a hobby operation in 1990 baking wedding and birthday cakes in the family kitchen using ordinary kitchen utensils by our founding Director, Mrs Millicent Mathenge. This later turned into a Sole-proprietor business with little or no hired labour. Her love for cakes was phenomenon which evolved to a passion for baking that was unstoppable. She would bake cakes and share with family, friends and neighbours. This was her side hustle when in University of Nairobi where she graduated from in 1990. Baking was more of a craze then such that we even had food kiosks in the Nairobi show ground and she never looked for employment as a result.

Humble Beginnings.

The first significant sale to an established business was KShs 5,779.00 (this was a whole month delivery) which was made to Uchumi Supermarkets Ltd. It was after this first successful launch sale to a supermarket. that she really got inspired and transferred the factory and business operations from the residential premises to the N.C.C.K Stalls in Makadara hamsa on Rabai Road, off Jogoo Road after receiving confirmed regular orders. As we did not have our own transport fleet to make deliveries over this period, we either borrowed vehicles from friends or hired the transport, including hiring of bicycle salesmen.

Growth.

For a period of about 5 years we continued our production and made supplies to Supermarkets and kiosks in Nairobi only. Being in a market set-up helped our cash flow due to walk-in customers. Our bakery equipments comprised of wood fired ovens and batter mixing was basically manual using wooden spatulas. We branched out of Nairobi and opened a branch in Nakuru which was closed within two years in 1997 due to the political clashes then. Despite all this our Nairobi business unit grew steadily. At this time, the country experienced acute electric power shortages, but our advantage was that we were using firewood in our ovens. There was acute shortage of maize flour and therefore people used to mill maize in this locality and most of them became our customers and brand ambassadors. Many were awed by the fact that cakes were made in wood fired ovens. KTN had to experience and covered this new phenomenon. Please watch the TV Feature..

Baking with a difference.

We used to work 24 hours a day and insecurity was unheard of that we only ‘tried’ to close the bakery door during the bomb blast in 1998, but the door broke down and we never closed. This marked our take-off period and business was good! We hired staff and even the banks noticed us, we even took loans to buy our vehicles from KCB. With the new growth we moved to larger premises in Kariobangi light industries. Our drive has been our mission to give our customers (we are the first customers) the very best based on continuous improvement.
